Giraffe

Giraffes are long-necked mammals of Africa, with very long legs and a coat pattern of brown patches. They are the tallest of all land animals, and are very common in grasslands of East Africa. These animals grow to almost their full height by four years of age but gain weight until they are seven or eight. Their tails can be quite long, up to a meter in length. They enjoy eating leaves especially from acacia trees.
